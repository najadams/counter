# MARKETING.md

> Operating rulebook for Protocol & Transfer Advisory (PTA) publicity, content, and demand generation.
> Companion to `SEO.md`. LinkedIn-first. Account-based. Premium tone, never UGC tone.

---

## 0. Strategic posture (read this first)

PTA's buyers are **named, findable, and small in number**: ~200–500 multinational subsidiaries operating in Ghana, plus the in-house counsel, country managers, finance directors, and compliance leads inside them. There are likely fewer than 1,500 individual decision-makers worth reaching in the entire market.

This dictates everything:

- **Reach is not the goal.** Authority in front of the *right* 1,500 people is the goal.
- **Distribution channel hierarchy:** LinkedIn organic > Direct outreach > Earned media > Speaking > SEO > Referrals > LinkedIn paid > everything else.
- **Channels we do NOT use:** TikTok, Instagram Reels (as a primary channel), Facebook ads, X/Twitter as a primary channel, YouTube ads, billboards, radio.
- **Brand discipline:** charcoal/gold, Cormorant Garamond + DM Sans + DM Mono, zero emoji, zero border-radius, zero purple. Every public asset honours this — including a LinkedIn post.

## 1. The five content pillars

Every public post falls into one of these five buckets. If a draft doesn't fit a pillar, it doesn't ship.

| # | Pillar | What it is | Who posts it | Frequency |
|---|--------|------------|--------------|-----------|
| 1 | **Regulatory Intelligence** | What changed this week at GIPC, BoG, GRA, GRA-TPU, Registrar-General, SEC, Data Protection Commission. Plain reading of the implication. | Lawyer partner (primary), Naj (secondary) | 2x weekly |
| 2 | **Case Patterns** | Anonymised lessons from real engagements — "Three reasons GIPC rejected a TTA filing last month." Never names of clients. | Lawyer partner | 1x weekly |
| 3 | **Sector Deep Dives** | Compliance landscape for one sector — mining, telecom, fintech, FMCG, oil & gas — in one post. | Either | 1x bi-weekly |
| 4 | **Frameworks & Tools** | "Here is the 7-step GIPC TTA checklist." Carousels and PDFs. Saves and shares. | Naj (primary, you build the carousels) | 1x weekly |
| 5 | **POV / Predictions** | Founder-voice opinion. Where is Ghana's TTA regime going. Why GIPC's filing volume is up 40%. | Either, in their own voice | 1x bi-weekly |

**Pillar mix per week:** 2 Regulatory + 1 Case + 1 Framework + 1 of {Sector, POV} = **5 posts/week across both partners.**

## 3. The five post formats

### 3.1 Insight post (text only, 150–300 words)

The workhorse. 80% of posts.

**Structure:**

```
[HOOK — one line. A claim, a number, or a question that stops scrolling.]

[Empty line.]

[CONTEXT — 2 sentences. What changed, what was unclear, what most people get wrong.]

[BODY — 3–5 short paragraphs. One idea per paragraph. Concrete, not abstract.
Use a regulation citation if relevant: "Under Section 30 of GIPC Act 865..."]

[IMPLICATION — 1–2 sentences. So what. What should a GC or country manager do differently on Monday.]

[CTA — one line, soft. "If your Ghana entity has a TTA up for renewal in Q4, the rules just moved." Never "DM me." Never "Book a call."]
```

**Hook examples that work for PTA:**
- "GIPC rejected 31% of TTA filings last quarter. The reasons are not what most counsel think."
- "There is one clause in the standard intra-group services agreement that triggers an automatic GRA transfer pricing review. Most multinationals in Ghana still have it."
- "Ghana's L.I. 1547 turns 33 next month. It is also the most-misread piece of subsidiary legislation in the country."

**Rules:**
- No emoji. None.
- No "hot take" language. No "Unpopular opinion." No "Here's a thread."
- No first-line gimmicks like "1." or "→" as the opening character.
- Line breaks are formatting. Use them. LinkedIn collapses dense paragraphs.
- Maximum one external link, placed in the first comment, never in the post body (LinkedIn down-ranks posts with body links).

### 3.2 Carousel post (8–10 slides, PDF document upload)

Highest save and share rate on LinkedIn. Use 2x/month minimum.

**Structure:**

| Slide | Purpose | Word count |
|-------|---------|------------|
| 1 — Cover | Hook + PTA mark. Title is the entire value prop. | <12 words |
| 2 — Premise | Why this matters. Who it's for. | <40 words |
| 3 — Context | The regulation / problem in one paragraph. | <50 words |
| 4–8 — Body | One point per slide. Number them. | <50 words each |
| 9 — Summary | The 5-point recap. | <60 words |
| 10 — CTA | "PTA helps multinationals operating in Ghana navigate TTA and GIPC compliance. ptadvisory.co" | <30 words |

**Design system for slides (matches `ptadvisory.co`):**
- Canvas: 1080 × 1350 px (LinkedIn's recommended portrait carousel)
- Background: `#0A0C10` (charcoal) — primary, OR `#FAF7F2` (cream) for variation
- Text colour: `#F4F1EC` on dark, `#0A0C10` on light
- Accent: `#C9A84C` (gold) — used for slide numbers, a single underline, dividing rules
- Display: Cormorant Garamond (italic for emphasis only)
- Body: DM Sans
- Meta/numbers: DM Mono
- Borders: zero radius. Sharp corners only.
- No drop shadows. No gradients. No icons except the PTA mark.

See `/linkedin-post-templates.html` (companion file) for visual reference.

### 3.3 Document post (PDF, 2–6 pages)

Long-form deep dive. Use 1x/month. Highest authority signal LinkedIn offers.

**Template:**
- Page 1: cover (same as carousel cover, larger)
- Pages 2–5: the analysis, with pull quotes and a sidebar with the relevant statute text
- Final page: PTA contact block + the lawyer partner's bio + Naj's bio

**Topics that earn this format:** an entire regulatory regime mapped, a sector compliance landscape, an annual outlook.

### 3.4 Video post (60–90s, talking head)

One partner on camera, professionally lit, branded lower-third. Subtitles burned in (85% of LinkedIn video is watched muted).

**Use sparingly** — 1x every 2–3 weeks. Reserved for high-signal moments: a regulatory shift, a year-end outlook, a single sharp insight worth a face.

This is where Higgsfield's avatar-led approach can substitute if shooting real video is the bottleneck — but the avatar must be of a real partner (Soul Character trained on real photos), not a stock avatar.

### 3.5 Comment-as-content

A 100–200 word substantive comment on a post by:
- A GC at a Ghana subsidiary (MTN, Bolt, Standard Chartered, AngloGold, Tullow, etc.)
- A Big 4 Ghana partner
- A Bank of Ghana / GIPC / GRA official
- A Ghana business journalist (B&FT, Joy Business, The Africa Report Ghana correspondent)

Two of these per week, each. They surface PTA to that person's network without the post needing to be ours.

**Rule:** never sell in a comment. Add a fact, a regulation citation, a clarification, or a contrarian observation. Sign-offs aren't needed — the byline does the work.

## 5. Beyond LinkedIn — the rest of the publicity machine

LinkedIn is the engine. These five channels are the multipliers.

### 5.1 Direct outreach with insight hooks

You have drafted emails for **Adiza Sefiyanu (PZ Cussons), Antoinette Kwofie + Stephen Blewett (MTN Ghana), Baaba Hammond (Bolt Ghana)**. Rework them before sending. Every outreach email must lead with a *specific* insight tied to a known fact about their entity — not a generic pitch.

**Insight-hook formula:**

```
Subject: [Specific regulatory observation] — [their company name] [their function]

[Line 1 — observation tied to a public fact about them]
"Noted [Company]'s [specific filing / announcement / movement] in [month].
[One-sentence regulatory implication they probably haven't fully internalised.]"

[Line 2 — the insight]
"Under [Section X of Act Y], that triggers [specific consequence].
We've seen [N] similar filings hit [specific issue] this year."

[Line 3 — the offer]
"PTA offers a 30-minute compliance audit. If useful, [link to 3 slots].
If not, no follow-up."

[Sign-off]
Naj Adams Lambon
Protocol & Transfer Advisory
ptadvisory.co
```

Track every outreach in a Supabase table or even a Google Sheet — `prospect | company | role | sent_at | opened | replied | meeting_booked | outcome`.

### 5.2 Earned media — the publication ladder

Target order. Land tier 1 before pitching tier 2.

| Tier | Outlets | What you pitch |
|------|---------|----------------|
| 1 — Ghana business press | Business & Financial Times, Joy Business, Citi Business News, Graphic Business | Quote-on-deadline regulatory commentary. Reach out to specific journalists, not the editor desk. |
| 2 — Pan-African business press | The Africa Report (Ghana correspondent), African Business Magazine, Bloomberg Africa, Reuters Africa | Bylined analytical piece (1,000–1,400 words) on TTA / GIPC regime |
| 3 — Industry verticals | Ghana Chamber of Mines newsletter, AmCham Ghana publications, Ghana Bar Association journal | Sector-specific bylined piece |
| 4 — International | Financial Times Africa, The Economist Africa coverage, Lexology, Mondaq | Year-end Ghana regulatory outlook, co-bylined |

**Pitch template for tier 1:**

```
Subject: Ghana regulatory comment — [topic] — [your name], Protocol & Transfer Advisory

[Reporter first name],

[One-sentence relevant observation tied to a story they recently wrote OR a story that's clearly developing.]

If useful for [a specific upcoming angle], I can offer a written comment under our managing partner's name at Protocol & Transfer Advisory. Bio at ptadvisory.co.

Three angles we could comment on:
1. [Angle 1]
2. [Angle 2]
3. [Angle 3]

Happy to send written quotes within 24 hours of confirmation.

Naj Adams Lambon
+233 555 547 998
```

**Goal:** one tier-1 placement within 60 days. Two within 90.

### 5.3 Speaking circuit — the rooms that matter

Apply / sponsor / attend, in priority order:

| Venue | Engagement type | Why |
|-------|-----------------|-----|
| AmCham Ghana monthly luncheons | Speaking slot | Direct access to US multinational subsidiaries |
| Ghana-British Chamber of Commerce events | Speaking + sponsor | UK multinationals (PZ Cussons, Standard Chartered, Tullow) |
| French-Ghanaian Chamber | Sponsor first, then speak | French multinationals (Bolloré, AXA, BIC) |
| GIPC investor events | Attend + table | The regulator's own room — you should be in it |
| Ghana Bar Association annual conference | Speaking slot (lawyer partner) | Referral pipeline from litigation-focused firms |
| Africa Business Convention (Accra editions) | Speaking | Cross-border M&A advisors |

**Lead time:** speaking slots are typically pitched 3–6 months out. Start outreach in month 1.

### 5.4 Strategic referral partnerships

Beyond Firmus and Globetrotters, formalise 3–4 more. Target profiles:

1. **Big 4 Ghana tax partners** (KPMG, Deloitte, EY, PwC) — they over-refer compliance-only work they don't want to scope. One coffee with each tax partner = pipeline.
2. **Boutique litigation firms in Accra** — they don't do transactional or regulatory; they refer it out.
3. **International law firms with Ghana practices** (Bowmans, ENS, DLA Piper Africa group) — they refer locally for local law opinions.
4. **Investment banks / corporate finance advisors** in Accra (Databank, Black Star, IC Securities) — they hit TTA/GIPC issues during M&A diligence and need a local advisor on speed-dial.

Pitch the partnership simply: "We send you litigation and contentious work. You send us TTA/GIPC and compliance. Quarterly catch-ups."

### 5.5 LinkedIn ads (later, not now)

Activate only after:
- 12+ weeks of consistent organic posting
- 1,500+ followers on your personal account
- One earned-media placement landed

Then run a small budget ($300–500/month) on **two campaign types only:**

1. **Document download campaign** — promote the best-performing carousel or PDF, gated behind a soft email capture. Targeting: "Legal Counsel" OR "Compliance Director" OR "Finance Director" OR "Country Manager" + company list (MTN, Vodafone Ghana, Tullow, AngloGold, Newmont, Tigo, Bolt, Uber, Heineken, Diageo, Nestlé, Unilever, PZ Cussons, Bolloré, AXA, MasterCard, Visa, ABSA, Standard Chartered, Stanbic — build the list from GIPC's published register).
2. **Thought leadership boost** — boost the single best-performing organic post per month to the same audience.

Never run lead-gen forms. Never run "Book a call." The campaign objective is **profile visits and follows**, not conversions.
